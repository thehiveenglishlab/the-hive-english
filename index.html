📚 Lesson Sample: “Dozing Off Danger”

Topic: The Hidden Risks of Sleep Deprivation
Level: Intermediate to Upper-Intermediate
Format: Listen – Learn – Talk

🎧 Listen: Dozing Off Danger - YouTube

🧠 Vocabulary

Deprivation: lack of something essential
Example: Chronic sleep deprivation can lead to serious health issues.
Red flag: warning sign
Example: Frequent yawning during the day is a red flag for sleep deprivation.
Impair: to damage or weaken
Example: Sleepiness can impair workplace performance.
Microsleep: very short unintended nap
Example: Drivers may lose control due to microsleep episodes.
Sedentary: sitting or inactive
Example: Falling asleep during sedentary tasks is a sign of deprivation.

🗣️ Discussion Questions

1. Have you ever almost fallen asleep without knowing? When?
2. Have you noticed a red flag in your health? What did you do?
3. Is sleep important to you? Do you prioritize it?
4. What happens when you sleep well?
5. Have you ever been too tired to focus? What helped?
6. What do people in Korea do to sleep better?
7. How do you make your room better for sleeping?
8. Do you use your phone before bed? Does it affect sleep?
9. Have you felt sleepy while driving/riding? What can help?
10. Do people in Korea talk about sleep problems?
11. Does caffeine affect your sleep?
12. How could your school or unit improve sleep for others?

✏️ Mini Activity (Optional)

Match the vocabulary to images or real-life situations. For example:
"Microsleep" – A person asleep on a train with a book still open.

🔍 Practice Page

  • Fill in the blanks
  • Match words to meanings
  • Write a short paragraph: Describe a time you were sleepy at school or work. What helped you wake up?
